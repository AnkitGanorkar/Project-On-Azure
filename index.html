Performance Based

Objectives and Metrics: Performance-based marketing campaigns are driven by specific objectives and measurable metrics. These metrics can vary depending on the goals of the campaign, but common examples include sales revenue, lead generation, customer acquisition, conversion rates, return on investment (ROI), or specific actions such as clicks or form submissions.

Payment Structure: In performance-based marketing, the payment structure is typically based on a predetermined agreement between the client and the marketing service provider. The compensation may be based on a percentage of sales or revenue generated, a fixed amount per lead or conversion, or a combination of different metrics. The payment is made once the agreed-upon results or milestones are achieved.

  • Results-oriented: Performance-based marketing focuses on achieving specific measurable outcomes, such as increased sales, higher conversion rates, or improved ROI. The success of the marketing efforts is determined by the actual results generated.
